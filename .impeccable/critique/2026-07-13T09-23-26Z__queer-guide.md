## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Not template slop — the monochrome editorial system (Space Grotesk display, black/white discipline, "ISSUE #340" newspaper conceits) is opinionated and recognizably designed. Two tells persist: eyebrow-kicker-on-every-module (HAPPENING / SHOP / KNOW BEFORE YOU GO / EXPLORE / CONTEXT on literally every section of every page — sanctioned as a metadata-label convention, but at this saturation it reads as scaffolding grammar, not voice) and the same abstract paint-marbling stock image recycled as hero across venues, events, and marketplace collections (Tom's Bar, "Berlin" Argentina, Budapest Pride, "Pride essentials" all wear the same swirl). The deeper failure is the product-register trust test: the chrome earns trust, then the data betrays it — €NaN prices, impossible flights, Burger King among Berlin's queer places, every event badged FEATURED. A Linear/Airbnb-fluent user trusts the shell and bounces at the third impossible number.

**Deterministic scan:** 8 findings across src/pages + 7 component dirs — all 8 triaged FALSE-POSITIVE (3 sanctioned-convention): a comment-line img match (CMSRoutePage.tsx:35), three `rounded-none border-b-2` underline-tab actives (Community/People/ProfilePage — standard monochrome nav), four font-family hits (Pattern Library scoped mirror + intentional Inter in print booklet and MapLibre popups). The ESLint design-system enforcement is working: the codebase is deterministically clean. Bonus catch: pattern-library.css:5 `@import` of Google Fonts is the app's only remote font import and prod CSP blocks it — dead weight, silently failing in production.

**Visual overlays:** Injection attempted on prod, blocked by CSP (`script-src 'self' …`, localhost not allowed; onerror confirmed in console). No user-visible overlay exists. Fallback objective evidence: 0 console errors, 0 document h-overflow, 0 broken images on /, /city/berlin, /events, /news — and 1 broken image on /marketplace (supergayunderwear.com Shopify CDN, naturalWidth=0, 717×350 visible slot, empty alt — the known ORB/no-referrer merchant-image class).

## Overall Impression

The design system is the strongest part of the product and the data is the weakest. The shell — monochrome editorial discipline, safety-first country pages, best-in-class error boundaries — is real design work. But this is a trust product for an audience whose stakes are legal risk, and the render layer forwards raw pipeline output: fabricated flights, NaN prices, misfiled venues, crashing spotlight links. The single biggest opportunity is a hard product rule: **safety-grade data or the module doesn't render.**

One meta-finding: the anon prod homepage served the map-shell variant, not the magazine front page shipped in #2055 — worth verifying which shell production is actually serving (flag/env dependent).

## What's Working

1. **The rights & safety layer is the real product.** ILGA criminalisation/protection matrix, dated, honest severity banners ("Dangerous", "Welcoming"), and the gated-content notice that explains *why* venues are hidden in criminalizing countries. Category-best safety UX.
2. **Failure-state craft.** Crash page offers search, recently-viewed, and four jump destinations; empty states teach (90-day visitor-signal decay explained). Someone designed failure.
3. **Editorial voice where humans wrote it.** Marketplace curation copy, brand stories, guide titles — direct, specific, on-brand, exactly the CLAUDE.md copy voice.

## Priority Issues

1. **[P0] Spotlight event detail crashes with no recovery** — `/events/san-diego-pride-2026`, linked as SPOTLIGHT + first card on /events. Error boundary catches it; Try Again does nothing (Budapest loads fine → data-dependent). **Why:** the most-promoted item on the events surface is a dead end. **Fix:** reproduce with that payload; per-section guards so one bad field degrades a module, not the route. → `/impeccable harden`
2. **[P0] Flight module fabricates routes and prices** — every country/city page: "ZRH → ROM" codes retitled "Zurich to Riyadh — Direct 1h35m — €35" (physically impossible); "€NaN" ×4 on Berlin. **Why:** impossible numbers next to safety data poison trust in the safety data. **Fix:** bind destination IATA from the page entity; hide module on mismatch/NaN. → `/impeccable harden`
3. **[P1] City pages claim "Rights data is not available" while the country page has it** — Berlin, under "KNOW BEFORE YOU GO / Safety & rights". **Why:** the core promise fails at the most-visited level. **Fix:** inherit the national ILGA block (page already notes it applies nationally). → `/impeccable harden`
4. **[P1] Venue & event detail pages have zero horizontal gutter** — `container mx-auto py-8` without `px-*`; h1, score ring, TYPE card touch x=0 at 1280 and 375. City/country pages are fine. **Why:** every entity page looks broken. **Fix:** add the missing padding token. → `/impeccable polish`
5. **[P1] Entity noise reaches every public rail** — Burger King/WeWork/Vodafone among Berlin queer places; Germany's Cities module lists Salzwedel + Oetz (Austria!) but not Berlin; TXL (closed 2020) in airports; raw "POLITIKER/IN" occupation strings; "similar" events matched by first letter (Budapest → Bucharest, Belgrade, Brussels, Bratislava, Bangkok). **Why:** truth-engine scores exist but don't gate rendering. **Fix:** public-surface quality gate (min score + category allowlist) on rails; i18n-map occupations. → `/impeccable harden` + pipeline work
6. **[P1] /news lead story unreadable** — near-black headline + excerpt over dark photo, no scrim (confirmed independently by both assessors). **Fix:** force scrim + `text-background` treatment on the lead card. → `/impeccable polish`
7. **[P2] Marketplace: broken merchant image at a 717×350 slot + inconsistent 18+ gating** — supergayunderwear.com image dead (known ORB class); explicit BDSM items in default "New this week" rail without 18+ treatment while other items carry the tag. → `/impeccable harden` + pipeline
8. **[P2] High-stakes tone collapse on criminalizing-country pages** — cheerful "Plan a trip to Saudi Arabia" CTA, GetYourGuide upsell, €35 fake flights, celebrity-gossip news on a death-penalty page; Berlin's "Next leg" suggests Mecca/Jeddah with flag emoji and no risk badge. **Fix:** risk-conditional page composition (suppress upsells, badge risky suggestions). → `/impeccable clarify` + `/impeccable harden`
